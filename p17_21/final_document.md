## 3.1    Reporting Process


<!-- Entity: E007 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

Approver: MANAGEMENT


<!-- Entity: E008 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

Effective Since: 01- 04- 2003


<!-- Entity: E009 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

During office hours


<!-- Entity: E010 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

The Master must report all emergencies by telephone and email to the Head Office of DANAOS Shipping Co. Ltd (DPA) as soon as possible.


<!-- Entity: E011 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

Office normal working hours are Monday to Friday 09:00 to 18:00 hrs.


<!-- Entity: E012 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

Outside office hours


<!-- Entity: E013 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

When an emergency occurs outside normal working hours or during the weekend or during public holidays the Master must call by telephone any of the following personnel listed below and follow up with email:


<!-- Entity: E014 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

 D.P.A./SQE Director or


<!-- Entity: E015 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

 Operation Director   or


<!-- Entity: E016 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

 Technical Director


<!-- Entity: E017 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

ALL TELEPHONE NUMBERS OF THE COMPANY'S EMERGENCY RESPONSE TEAM ARE GIVEN IN CHAPTER 7


<!-- Entity: E018 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

Important


<!-- Entity: E019 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

Masters are reminded of their legal obligation to report incident to Flag, Port and Coast State Authorities, after first advising the Managers.


<!-- Entity: E020 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

<!-- Entity: E021 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

<!-- Entity: E022 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 1 -->

## Important Notes:


<!-- Entity: E073 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 3 -->

The Master must report incidents involving but not limited to Collision, Grounding, Stranding, Fire, Explosion, Hull Failure, Excessive list, as mentioned in EPM Chapter 10, which have caused an emergency situation as soon as practicable.


<!-- Entity: E074 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 3 -->

Messages related to the same incident must be numbered sequentially, i.e. the first message is 'Urgent one', the next is 'Urgent two' and so on.

b- Ships involved in pollution incidents.


<!-- Entity: E089 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 4 -->

The Master of a ship involved in pollution incident, in suspected pollution incident or in incident that could  give  rise  to  pollution  incident,  must  initiate  the  emergency  reporting  process,  soonest  possible, refer to Section 2 of the vessel's individual Shipboard Oil Pollution Emergency Plan ( SOPEP) where all reporting and action requirements are detailed.


<!-- Entity: E090 | Type: EntityType.TEXT | Page: 4 -->

In all cases of Emergency which could affect the Vessel's, Crew and Cargo Safety, it is of prime importance the Master must conduct Breathalyser tests and obtain urine samples for analysis from the Officers and crew involved.
